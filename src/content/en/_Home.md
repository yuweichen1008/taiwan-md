# Taiwan Knowledge Hub

An island of 36,197 square kilometers—smaller than Switzerland, larger than Belgium—produces 65% of the world's advanced semiconductor chips. This statistic alone is a riddle. Why Taiwan? Why here?

> **📝 Editor's Note**
> 
> Taiwan.md isn't an encyclopedia; it's a curated space. We use stories to understand data,
> details to touch reality. Every article seeks to answer the same question:
> **Why is Taiwan worth your time to understand?**

In 2024, 23.4 million Taiwanese created $795.6 billion GDP, with per capita income of $33,983—surpassing South Korea and Japan. The story behind these numbers is more fascinating than the figures themselves: from 1950s agricultural society to 1980s "Taiwan Miracle" to today's position as the heart of global technology supply chains.

No oil, no abundant minerals, not even stable geology—an average of 3.7 perceptible earthquakes annually. Yet this island used education, innovation, and a unique "fighting spirit" to write one of the 20th century's most incredible development stories.

## 🗺️ Explore Taiwan's 12 Dimensions

### 🧠 [[Semiconductor Industry]]: Genesis Code of the Sacred Mountain

**Why did Taiwan become the semiconductor kingdom?** In 1987, TSMC founder Morris Chang made what seemed "crazy" at the time: focus only on foundry services, no brands. 30 years later, this decision gave Taiwan control of the global tech supply chain's throat. From cleanrooms to talent cultivation, from policy support to industrial clusters—how did Taiwan build a semiconductor empire on sand?

### 🏛️ [[History]]: From Peripheral Island to Global Focus

**What forces shaped today's Taiwan?** Dutch East India Company, Zheng dynasty, Qing administration, Japanese rule, KMT retreat—each regime left its mark on this island. February 28 Incident, White Terror, Martial Law Lifting, democratization—how did Taiwan transition from authoritarianism to democracy, becoming the democratic beacon of the Chinese-speaking world?

### 🍜 [[Food Culture]]: Civilization Density Behind Night Markets

**Why is Taiwanese cuisine so diverse?** A bowl of beef noodle soup contains military dependents' village stories, oyster omelets carry Hoklo memories, xiaolongbao fuses Jiangzhe craftsmanship. From night markets to Michelin stars, from grandma's recipes to innovative cuisine, Taiwanese food reflects not just taste buds but 400 years of cultural convergence evidence.

### 🎭 [[Arts & Culture]]: From Traditional Crafts to International Stage

**Why does Taiwanese art shine internationally?** Cloud Gate Dance Theatre showed the world Eastern body aesthetics, Hou Hsiao-hsien conquered Cannes with long-take poetry, Che-Yu Wu's generative art swam to Venice Biennale. From puppetry to contemporary art, from Hakka bayin to pop music—how does the island nurture creativity that transcends language?

### 🎵 [[Music]]: From Folk Songs to Mandarin Pop Hub

**Why did Taiwan become the center of Mandarin music?** Mayday performed at Madison Square Garden, Jay Chou rewrote Mandarin pop grammar, Jody Chiang made Taiwanese songs beloved by youth again. From indigenous tribal sounds to international music festivals—how did Taiwanese music move from periphery to center?

### 💻 [[Technology & Innovation]]: From OEM Kingdom to Innovation Island

**How did Taiwan evolve from "world factory" to "innovation base"?** Foxconn transformed from a small connector company to global OEM leader, Gogoro redefined electric scooters, Digital Minister Audrey Tang made government-citizen collaboration possible. Open source culture, digital democracy, social innovation—what kind of tech story is Taiwan writing?

### 🌿 [[Ecology & Environment]]: Small Island, Big Nature's Biological Miracle

**Why is Taiwan's biodiversity so rich?** Less than 40,000 square kilometers, yet over 18,000 native species. From sea-level mangroves to 3,952-meter Yushan, vertical distribution created a world-class ecological treasure. Formosan black bears, landlocked salmon, Taiwan blue magpies—how do these endemic species survive on a rapidly developing island?

### 👥 [[Notable People]]: Taiwanese Who Influenced the World

**What kind of people shaped Taiwan?** Chiang Ching-kuo promoted Ten Major Construction Projects, Lee Teng-hui completed democratic transition, Ang Lee brought Eastern cinema to the Oscars, Morris Chang built the semiconductor empire. From politicians to artists, entrepreneurs to athletes—how did these figures change historical trajectory at critical moments?

### 🗺️ [[Geography]]: Island Terrain Coexisting with Natural Disasters

**How to build civilization on a disaster-prone island?** Located at the junction of Eurasian and Philippine Sea plates, Taiwan faces annual typhoons, earthquakes, torrential rains. Yet this violent geological activity also created high mountains and deep valleys, hot springs and geothermal energy, fertile plains. How do Taiwanese harmoniously coexist with natural disasters?

### 🏛️ [[Politics & Society]]: East Asian Democratic Experiment Model

**How can Chinese society move toward democracy?** From martial law to its lifting, from single-party to multi-party competition, from authoritarianism to democracy—Taiwan completed political transition in 30 years. Sunflower Movement, marriage equality, transitional justice—how is Taiwan's new generation deepening democracy?

### 💼 [[Economic Development]]: From Agricultural Island to Tech Powerhouse

**What's the truth behind the "Taiwan Miracle"?** 1960-1990, Taiwan's economy grew at an annual average of 8.7%, dubbed the leader of the "Four Asian Tigers." Land reform released rural productivity, export orientation established industrial foundation, science parks nurtured high-tech industries. How were three industrial transformations completed within one generation?

### 🏮 [[Daily Life Culture]]: National Health Insurance & Convenience Store Life

**What defines Taiwanese-style living?** One health insurance card covers all, world's highest 7-Eleven density, scooter riders navigating urban jungles, night markets that never sleep. From MRT courtesy culture to "garbage doesn't touch ground," from queuing aesthetics to human warmth—how does the island maintain human temperature in the modernization process?

## 💡 Did You Know?

**Taiwan has the world's highest convenience store density**—an average of one store per 2,120 people, denser than Japan. But this isn't just a commercial phenomenon; it's a microcosm of urban lifestyle.

**Taiwan is the world's only region with both democratic institutions and Traditional Chinese characters**—this uniqueness places Taiwan in a special position in cross-strait relations, international politics, and cultural identity.

**Taiwanese songs are transforming from "uncle songs" to "hipster favorites"**—new generation bands like EggPlantEgg and Sorry Youth reinterpret mother tongue music with modern arrangements, proving traditional culture can be reborn in the globalization era.
