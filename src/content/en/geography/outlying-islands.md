# Outlying Islands and Maritime Culture

## 30-Second Overview

Taiwan is surrounded by a constellation of outlying islands, each forming part of a rich maritime cultural sphere. The Penghu Archipelago is celebrated for its basalt geology and ocean tourism. Kinmen and Matsu preserve military heritage and traditional Fujianese architecture. Green Island and Lanyu showcase indigenous ocean civilization. And Little Liuqiu is a coral reef ecological paradise. These islands possess not only unique natural landscapes but have also developed ways of life in harmony with the sea -- making them essential windows into the diversity of Taiwan's maritime civilization.

**Major Outlying Islands:** Penghu Archipelago, Kinmen County, Lienchiang County (Matsu), Green Island, Lanyu (Orchid Island), Little Liuqiu

## Why It Matters

Taiwan's outlying islands are microcosms of maritime Taiwan. Each island, shaped by its unique geographic position, geological conditions, and historical trajectory, has developed distinct maritime cultural characteristics. These islands are not only treasure troves of biodiversity but also living repositories of maritime cultural tradition.

For understanding Taiwan, island culture demonstrates how an island-dwelling people developed sustainable living wisdom within the constraints of limited land and a demanding ocean environment. These lessons carry profound relevance for modern societies confronting climate change and rising sea levels.

## Penghu Archipelago: A Basalt Ocean Kingdom

### Geography

**Composition:** 90 islands large and small, total area 127 square kilometers
**Population:** Approximately 106,000
**Geology:** Basalt plateaus with abundant sea-erosion landforms

The Penghu Archipelago sits in the middle of the Taiwan Strait and is Taiwan's largest island group. Its distinctive basalt geology has produced numerous world-class geological formations, including columnar jointing, sea caves, and wave-cut platforms.

**Signature Attractions:**

- **Double-Heart Stone Weir (Qimei):** An artistic crystallization of ancient fishing techniques
- **Daguoye Columnar Basalt:** A spectacular geological wonder
- **Kuibishan Moses Parting the Sea:** A natural tidal pathway
- **Blue Cave (Xiyu):** A sea cave that creates mesmerizing blue light effects

### Maritime Cultural Traditions

**Stone Weir Culture:** Penghu has the highest density of stone fish weirs in the world, with 574 still in existence. These weirs are fishing structures designed by early settlers who leveraged tidal patterns and fish behavior, demonstrating a deep understanding of marine ecology.

**Wind Culture:** Penghu's fierce northeast monsoon winds have produced a distinctive culture of living with the wind. Locals have developed adaptations in architecture, agriculture, and daily life to coexist with their gusty environment.

**Fishing Traditions:**

- **Anchovy Culture:** Penghu produces 80% of Taiwan's anchovies
- **Seaweed Harvesting:** Collecting and processing laver and agar
- **Sea Fishing Culture:** A rich tradition of offshore fishing techniques

### Tourism Development

**Penghu International Fireworks Festival:** Held annually from April to June, this event combines fireworks, music, and ocean vistas into a dazzling spectacle, and has become Penghu's signature tourism brand.

**Water Activities:** Snorkeling, diving, sailing, kayaking, and more take full advantage of Penghu's crystal-clear waters and coral reef ecosystems.

## Kinmen County: A Bastion of Southern Fujian Culture

### Historical and Cultural Background

**Area:** 151 square kilometers
**Population:** Approximately 140,000
**Defining Features:** Military heritage, Southern Fujian architecture, Kaoliang liquor culture

Kinmen's strategic geographic position made it a frontline during the era of cross-strait military confrontation, giving rise to a unique military heritage. At the same time, Kinmen preserves some of the most complete traditional Southern Fujian architecture and culture.

### Military Heritage

**Military Installations:**

- **Tunnel Systems:** Zhaishan Tunnel, Siwei Tunnel, and other underground military facilities
- **Bunkers:** Military fortifications scattered across the island
- **Anti-Airborne Stakes:** Beach-front military defense structures
- **Military Slogans:** Painted slogans like "Retake the Mainland" have become historical cultural landmarks

**Wartime Administration Period:** From 1956 to 1992, Kinmen operated under military governance, creating a unique social environment that is now recognized as important historical and cultural heritage.

### Southern Fujian Architecture

**Traditional Settlements:**

- **Shanhou Folk Culture Village:** A complete Southern Fujian architectural complex
- **Zhushan Settlement:** A village blending traditional and overseas Chinese architecture
- **Oucuo Settlement:** A well-preserved traditional Southern Fujian village

**Architectural Features:**

- **Swallowtail Ridge:** The signature roofline of Southern Fujian buildings
- **Horse-Back Gable Walls:** Serving both fireproofing and decorative functions
- **Stone and Wood Carvings:** Exquisite traditional craft ornamentation
- **Overseas Chinese Architecture:** A distinctive fusion of Western and Fujian styles built with remittances from abroad

### Kaoliang Liquor Culture

**Kinmen Kaoliang:** Brewed using Kinmen's premium groundwater and sorghum, this internationally renowned spirit is both Kinmen's most important industry and its cultural symbol.

**Distillery Tourism:** The Kinmen Distillery is open to visitors, showcasing the kaoliang production process and its cultural significance.

## Lienchiang County (Matsu): From Military Frontline to Artistic Rebirth

### Geography and Culture

**Composition:** Four townships -- Nangan, Beigan, Dongyin, and Juguang (Dongju and Xiju)
**Area:** 29.6 square kilometers
**Population:** Approximately 13,000
**Defining Features:** Military heritage, Eastern Fujian culture, Blue Tears phenomenon

The Matsu Islands sit at the mouth of the Min River in Fujian Province. Culturally, they belong to the Eastern Fujian cultural sphere, distinct from the Southern Fujian culture of Taiwan proper.

### Military Heritage

**Military Tunnels:** The 88 Tunnel (Nangan), Beihai Tunnel (Beigan), and others showcase astonishing feats of military engineering.

**Military Outposts:** Each island has densely packed military installations, most of which have now been converted to tourist attractions or art spaces.

### The Blue Tears Phenomenon

**Bioluminescence:** Dinoflagellates in the waters around Matsu emit blue fluorescent light when disturbed, creating the spectacular "Blue Tears" effect.

**Tourism Value:** The best viewing season runs from April to August, drawing large numbers of visitors on a near-pilgrimage.

### Eastern Fujian Culture

**Language:** The Matsu dialect belongs to the Eastern Min language family, distinct from Taiwan's Southern Min (Hokkien)
**Religion:** Mazu worship is especially prominent -- the goddess is the origin of the islands' name
**Architecture:** Traditional buildings primarily of granite, adapted to the local climate

## Green Island: Rebirth of a Prison Island

### Geography

**Area:** 15.1 square kilometers
**Population:** Approximately 3,000
**Geology:** Volcanic island surrounded by coral reefs

Green Island, originally named Fire Island, is a volcanic island southeast of Taiwan proper, encircled by coral reefs and teeming with marine life.

### Human Rights History

**Green Island Prison:** During the martial law era, this was where political prisoners were incarcerated. A Human Rights Memorial Park now stands on the site.

**White Terror:** Green Island is an important witness to Taiwan's White Terror period and today serves as a site for human rights education.

### Marine Ecology and Tourism

**Diving Paradise:** Rich coral reef ecosystems make Green Island one of Taiwan's premier diving destinations.

**Zhaori Hot Springs:** One of only a handful of saltwater hot springs in the world, where visitors can soak while taking in ocean views.

**Marine Protected Area:** A designated marine reserve protects the coral reef ecosystem.

## Lanyu (Orchid Island): The Tao People's Ocean Civilization

### Indigenous Maritime Culture

**Area:** 48 square kilometers
**Population:** Approximately 5,000 (about 4,000 are Tao people)
**Defining Features:** Tao culture, Flying Fish Festival, traditional boat culture

Lanyu is the island where Taiwan's most intact indigenous maritime civilization is preserved. The culture of the Tao people (formerly known as Yami) is inseparable from the ocean.

### Flying Fish Culture

**Flying Fish Festival:** Held annually from February to June, it encompasses elaborate rituals including the Fish-Calling Ceremony and the Storage Ceremony.

**Ecological Wisdom:** Through traditional calendars and taboo systems, the Tao maintain sustainable use of marine resources.

**Flying Fish Harvesting:** Governed by strict seasonal rules and prescribed catching methods.

### Traditional Boat Culture (Plank Boats)

**Hull Paintings:** Each boat features unique traditional patterns symbolizing tribal identity.

**Boatbuilding Techniques:** Constructed using traditional methods without nails, assembled entirely with mortise-and-tenon joinery.

**Maritime Rituals:** Boat-launching ceremonies involve elaborate traditional rites.

### Traditional Architecture

**Underground Houses:** Semi-subterranean dwellings designed to withstand typhoons.

**Cooling Platforms:** Important spaces for work and socializing.

**Building Materials:** Locally sourced stone and wood.

## Little Liuqiu: A Coral Reef Ecological Paradise

### Geography

**Area:** 6.8 square kilometers
**Population:** Approximately 13,000
**Defining Features:** Coral reef island, sea turtle ecology, tourism destination

Little Liuqiu is Taiwan's only coral reef island. Enjoying spring-like weather year-round and minimal typhoon interference, it is an ideal marine tourism destination.

### Marine Ecology

**Green Sea Turtles:** Little Liuqiu's waters have the highest sea turtle density in Taiwan, and green sea turtles are frequently spotted swimming nearby.

**Coral Reefs:** Rich coral reef ecosystems make the island perfect for snorkeling and diving.

**Marine Conservation:** The island actively promotes ocean conservation, including bans on single-use plastics.

### Tourism Development

**Island Circuit:** A scooter loop around the island takes just one to two hours, making it a popular choice for a quick getaway.

**Water Activities:** Snorkeling, diving, kayaking, stand-up paddleboarding, and other water sports are thriving.

**Eco-Tourism:** The island promotes responsible tourism to protect the marine environment.

## Shared Traits and Common Challenges

### Shared Cultural Characteristics

**Ocean Dependence:** Every outlying island has developed a culture of coexistence with the sea.

**Tight-Knit Communities:** Island life fosters close-knit social bonds.

**Environmental Adaptation:** Unique adaptations to sea winds, salt corrosion, and typhoons have shaped distinctive ways of living.

**External Influences:** Each island has been shaped by different outside cultures, resulting in unique cultural blends.

### Modernization Challenges

**Population Outflow:** Young people tend to move to the main island for opportunities, leaving behind aging populations.

**Industrial Transition:** A shift from traditional fishing to tourism-based economies.

**Environmental Protection:** Balancing tourism development with ecological preservation.

**Cultural Preservation:** Maintaining traditional culture amid modernization.

### Tourism Development Models

**Eco-Tourism:** Emphasizing environmental protection and sustainable development.

**Cultural Tourism:** Leveraging distinctive local cultural assets.

**Deep Travel:** Moving from superficial tourism toward immersive cultural experiences.

**Responsible Tourism:** Encouraging visitors to participate in environmental stewardship.

## The Value of Island Maritime Civilization

### Ecological Wisdom

Over generations of ocean living, Taiwan's island residents have accumulated rich ecological knowledge:

- **Seasonal Harvesting:** Aligning with the life cycles of marine organisms
- **Sustainable Fishing:** Developing catch methods that do not damage ecosystems
- **Environmental Adaptation:** Architecture, agriculture, and lifestyles adapted to the marine environment

### Cultural Diversity

Each island offers its own cultural character:

- **Penghu:** A classic example of Han maritime culture
- **Kinmen and Matsu:** Fusion of military heritage with Southern and Eastern Fujian culture
- **Green Island:** A dialogue between human rights history and marine ecology
- **Lanyu:** A fully preserved indigenous ocean civilization
- **Little Liuqiu:** A model for sustainable modern marine tourism

### Significance for Taiwan

Island culture is an essential component of maritime Taiwan, showcasing the diversity and depth of the nation's ocean civilization. These islands are not just parts of Taiwan's territory -- they are vital carriers of Taiwan's cultural identity and maritime consciousness.

## Climate Change and Future Challenges

### Sea Level Rise Threats

Outlying islands universally face the threat of rising sea levels, particularly lower-lying islands. Adapting to climate change is a critical challenge for island communities.

### Sustainable Development Strategies

- **Renewable Energy:** Developing wind and solar power
- **Desalination:** Addressing freshwater resource shortages
- **Circular Economy:** Reducing waste and improving resource efficiency
- **Eco-Tourism:** Developing tourism models that do not harm the environment

### Cultural Continuity

Finding the balance between economic development and cultural preservation during modernization remains a vital social issue for Taiwan's outlying islands.

## Tips for Visitors

### Best Travel Seasons

- **Penghu:** April to September; fireworks festival runs April to June
- **Kinmen and Matsu:** Pleasant year-round; cooler in autumn and winter
- **Green Island and Lanyu:** April to October; avoid the northeast monsoon season
- **Little Liuqiu:** Year-round; spring-like weather in all seasons

### Getting There

- **Flights:** Faster but more expensive; subject to weather disruptions
- **Ferries:** More affordable but longer; seasickness possible
- **Charters:** An option for group travel

### Responsible Travel

- **Environmental Protection:** Leave no trash behind and do not damage ecosystems
- **Cultural Respect:** Honor local cultures and traditions
- **Support Local:** Purchase local products and support the local economy
- **Sustainable Choices:** Choose environmentally friendly travel options

## Closing Reflections

Taiwan's outlying islands are essential windows into maritime civilization and island wisdom. Under the twin pressures of climate change and globalization, how these small islands maintain their cultural distinctiveness and ecological integrity while pursuing modern development is a question worth careful consideration.

The experiences of these islands offer important insights into how human communities can build sustainable civilizations with limited resources. The maritime wisdom found on these shores may prove to be a valuable reference as humanity faces the environmental challenges ahead.
