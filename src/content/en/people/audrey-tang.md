# Audrey Tang

Audrey Tang is a trailblazer who has redefined political participation in the digital age. From a self-taught programming prodigy at 14 to becoming the world's first openly transgender cabinet-level official, her life story embodies the diversity, inclusiveness, and innovative vitality of Taiwanese society. As a pioneer of the open government movement and co-founder of g0v (gov zero), Tang has reformed government operations through technical expertise while practicing the vision of digital democracy guided by her philosophy of "conservative anarchism" and the principle that "code is law." Hers is a modern tale of weaving a better society through code.

## Early Life and an Unconventional Upbringing

### A Gifted Child's Struggles and Awakening

On April 18, 1981, Audrey Tang was born in Taiwan under the birth name Tang Zong-han. She displayed extraordinary intelligence from a young age, with reports describing her IQ as exceeding 180, earning her the label of "Taiwan's computer prodigy." However, this giftedness did not translate into a smooth academic journey.

**Difficulties adapting to school:**
Being far more intellectually advanced than her peers, Tang faced serious difficulties interacting with classmates and teachers. She experienced bullying and could not adapt to the traditional education system. Over the course of nine years, she attended three kindergartens and six elementary schools -- a painful experience that prompted deep reflection on the education system.

**A mother's progressive approach to education:**
Tang's mother, Li Ya-qing, was an education reformer. Confronted with her daughter's struggles in school, she decided to implement a progressive home education approach. Li Ya-qing even took her two children to Germany to experience different educational environments, conducting in-depth research into alternative education methods and experimental schooling.

**The beginning of self-directed learning:**
After receiving a guaranteed admission offer to the prestigious Jianguo High School, Tang made a surprising decision: she chose to forgo traditional schooling entirely and pursue self-directed learning at home. This choice attracted considerable attention in Taiwanese society at the time and marked the beginning of her unique educational journey.

### Programming Awakening at 14

**First encounter with computers:**
At 14, Tang discovered programming, igniting a deep and lasting connection with the digital world. She not only mastered programming languages rapidly but also demonstrated a profound understanding of computer science and a capacity for innovation.

**A self-taught journey:**
Without systematic instruction from any teacher, Tang mastered the art of programming entirely through self-study. She read widely from technical documentation, participated in online discussions, and sought learning resources within internet communities. This experience of autonomous learning laid the philosophical foundation for her later advocacy of open education and knowledge sharing.

**Early technical achievements:**
While still a teenager, Tang was already contributing to open-source software development. Her technical talent and innovative thinking gradually earned her recognition within the programming community.

### Exploring Gender Identity and Coming Out

**Gender identity awakening:**
During her formative years, Tang gradually realized that her gender identity did not align with the sex assigned at birth. This process of self-discovery was not easy, requiring courage to confront societal prejudice and the potential misunderstanding of family and friends.

**A pivotal decision at 24:**
In 2005, at the age of 24, Tang publicly announced her identity as a transgender woman and underwent gender reassignment surgery. She changed her Chinese name from Tang Zong-han to Tang Feng and chose Audrey as her English name -- a decision that demanded tremendous courage and resolve.

**A major social breakthrough:**
Tang's public coming out carried significant symbolic meaning in Taiwanese society. Through her actions, she demonstrated that transgender individuals can play important roles in society, making a meaningful contribution to the LGBTQ+ rights movement in Taiwan.

**Family support:**
Fortunately, Tang's family provided her with full understanding and support. Her mother Li Ya-qing's open-minded attitude offered crucial emotional support during Tang's gender transition.

## Programming Career and Open-Source Contributions

Audrey Tang was an important contributor to the Perl programming language community. She participated in the development of several major Perl projects, making outstanding contributions particularly in the design and implementation of Perl 6.

**Launching the Pugs project:**

**Core contributions to Haskell:**

**Community leadership:**
Within the Haskell and Perl communities, Tang served not only as a technical contributor but also as a vital community leader. She organized conferences, drove project development, and mentored new developers, making significant contributions to the advancement of open-source software.

**Cross-disciplinary thinking:**
Tang's contributions extended beyond programming. She applied technological reasoning to the social sciences, political science, and other fields, skillfully translating abstract technical concepts into concrete social practices.

## g0v (Gov Zero): Pioneering Civic Technology

### The Founding and Philosophy of g0v

**A landmark initiative in 2012:**
In 2012, Tang and several like-minded colleagues co-founded g0v (pronounced "gov zero"), one of Taiwan's most important civic technology communities. The name replaces the "o" in "gov" with a "0," symbolizing the reimagining of government through the digital language of zeros and ones.

**The spirit of "hack, don't attack":**
g0v's core philosophy is "hack, don't attack" -- meaning rather than attacking existing systems, use technology to improve them. This constructive approach to civic participation drives social progress through practical action.

**Promoting open data:**
g0v is dedicated to promoting government open data, enabling citizens to more easily access and understand government information. The community developed various tools and platforms that transform dense government data into understandable visual charts.

**Experiments in participatory democracy:**
Through g0v, Tang and her partners experimented with various forms of participatory democracy. They believe technology can lower the barriers to civic participation, enabling more people to engage in discussions about public affairs.

### Notable Projects and Achievements

**Central government budget visualization:**
One of g0v's early landmark projects was the visualization of the central government budget. The original dense and impenetrable budget documents were transformed into interactive charts, allowing citizens to clearly see how their tax money was being spent.

**Legislative Yuan IVOD:**
This project organized and tagged video recordings of Legislative Yuan sessions, enabling citizens to quickly find discussions on specific issues and increasing the transparency of the legislative process.

**MoeDict:**
Tang helped develop MoeDict, an open Chinese dictionary platform. This project not only provided quality Chinese language learning resources but also demonstrated the value of open-source software in cultural preservation.

**Disaster information integration:**
During major disasters, the g0v community rapidly developed information integration platforms, consolidating data from multiple sources to help citizens stay informed of real-time conditions.

**Election-related projects:**
g0v developed multiple election-related projects, including candidate information aggregation and policy comparison tools, improving voters' access to information.

### International Influence and Expansion

**Connecting with global civic tech communities:**
g0v's success attracted international attention, becoming an important case study in the global civic technology movement. Civic tech communities from around the world visited Taiwan to learn from the g0v experience.

**Open Government Partnership:**
g0v's philosophy and practical experience provided an important foundation for Taiwan's participation in the international Open Government Partnership.

**Replicating the model:**
g0v's operational model has been replicated in other countries and regions, contributing to the growth of the global civic technology movement.

**Subject of academic research:**
g0v became the subject of numerous academic studies, with international scholars researching this Taiwan-born civic technology model.

## vTaiwan: An Innovative Experiment in Digital Democracy

### The Birth of the vTaiwan Platform

**The need for digital regulatory adaptation:**
In 2014, the rapid development of the digital economy posed enormous challenges to traditional regulations. Emerging internet services, the sharing economy, digital currencies, and other issues all required new regulatory frameworks.

**The need for multi-stakeholder dialogue:**
Facing complex digital issues, the government recognized the need for a platform where different stakeholders -- including businesses, civil society, and academic experts -- could engage in dialogue.

**vTaiwan's design philosophy:**
The vTaiwan platform, which Tang helped design, employed innovative digital participation mechanisms. Beyond simply providing information transparency, it established a structured dialogue process.

**Merging technology and democracy:**
vTaiwan demonstrated how to combine technological tools with democratic participation. The platform utilized various digital tools, including online voting, opinion mapping, and real-time discussion, enabling participants to fully express their views.

### Operational Mechanisms and Innovations

**A four-stage participation process:**
vTaiwan designed a four-stage participation process: proposal, opinion, deliberation, and legislation. Each stage features different participation methods and objectives, ensuring a comprehensive policy-making process.

**Pol.is opinion integration technology:**
The platform adopted Pol.is, an opinion integration tool capable of analyzing and visualizing large volumes of opinions, identifying areas of consensus and disagreement, and helping participants understand different perspectives.

**Combining online and offline engagement:**
vTaiwan incorporated not only online participation mechanisms but also regular in-person workshops and seminars, enabling participants from diverse backgrounds to exchange ideas face to face.

**An open and transparent process:**
The entire policy discussion process was open and transparent, allowing citizens to follow the progress of discussions and understand the basis for decisions at any time.

### Success Stories and Impact

**The Uber regulatory discussion:**
vTaiwan's most well-known success story involved the regulatory discussion around ride-sharing platforms like Uber. Through the platform's dialogue mechanisms, various parties reached a degree of consensus that provided important input for government policymaking.

**FinTech regulatory sandbox:**
vTaiwan also participated in policy discussions around fintech regulatory sandboxes, helping the government find a balance between encouraging innovation and managing risk.

**Digital economy regulatory adaptation:**
The platform continued to address various digital economy regulatory issues, serving as an important bridge for dialogue between government and civil society.

**An international best practice:**
vTaiwan's innovative approach earned international recognition and is regarded as a model of digital democracy, with numerous countries sending delegations to Taiwan to learn from the experience.

## Entering Government: The Innovative Role of Digital Minister

### The Historic Appointment of 2016

**The youngest minister without portfolio at 35:**
On October 1, 2016, the 35-year-old Audrey Tang accepted the invitation of Premier Lin Chuan to serve as Minister without Portfolio of the Executive Yuan, becoming the youngest person to hold the position and Taiwan's first "Digital Minister."

**A breakthrough for transgender representation in politics:**
Tang's appointment carried profound historical significance. She became the world's first openly transgender cabinet-level government official, establishing an important milestone for LGBTQ+ political participation.

**An unconventional political figure:**
Unlike traditional politicians, Tang had no party affiliation, entering government based on her technical expertise and civic engagement experience. Her appointment reflected the Tsai Ing-wen administration's commitment to diversity in governance.

**A new era for digital governance:**
Tang's appointment marked the beginning of a new chapter in Taiwan's digital governance, as the government sought to leverage her technical expertise and innovative thinking to advance digital economy and open government objectives.

### Revolutionizing the Way Government Works

**A pioneer in remote work:**
Upon entering government, Tang proposed a remote work model, forgoing a fixed office in favor of mobile working. This approach proved visionary when COVID-19 struck years later.

**Principles of openness and transparency:**
She insisted that all work meetings be publicly transcribed, unless they involved state secrets or personal privacy. This radical transparency brought an unprecedented level of openness to government operations.

**Collaboration over command:**
Tang's working style emphasized collaboration rather than top-down direction. Rather than giving orders as a superior, she partnered with various ministries to solve problems together.

**Balancing technology and humanity:**
Despite her powerful technical background, Tang consistently stressed that technology must serve human needs. Her policy thinking always placed people's feelings and needs first.

### Key Policy Initiatives and Achievements

**Advancing the digital economy:**
Tang actively promoted digital economy policies, including digital transformation, e-commerce, and fintech. She helped the government create a more conducive environment for digital economic development.

**Practicing open government:**
She brought her experience from g0v and vTaiwan into government, promoting various open government initiatives including open data, civic participation, and transparent governance.

**Youth engagement policies:**
Tang placed particular emphasis on youth participation, establishing mechanisms such as the Youth Advisory Committee to ensure young people's voices were heard by the government.

**Supporting social innovation:**
She promoted social innovation policies, supporting social enterprises and public interest organizations, seeking innovative approaches to addressing social challenges.

## Minister of Digital Affairs: Institutionalizing Digital Governance

### The Establishment of the Ministry

**The institutional need for digital governance:**
As digitalization accelerated, Taiwan needed a dedicated ministry to coordinate digital-related policies. The establishment of the Ministry of Digital Affairs marked the transition of digital governance from experimental to institutionalized.

**The historic moment of August 27, 2022:**
On August 27, 2022, the Ministry of Digital Affairs was officially established, with Audrey Tang appointed as its inaugural minister. This represented an important milestone in the history of Taiwan's government organization and a new peak in Tang's political career.

**Integrated digital governance:**
The Ministry consolidated digital-related functions previously scattered across various ministries, including telecommunications, cybersecurity, and the digital economy, establishing a more comprehensive digital governance framework.

**Meeting the challenge of global digital competition:**
Against the backdrop of intensifying global digital competition, the Ministry's establishment represented an important step in enhancing Taiwan's digital competitiveness.

### Major Policies During Her Tenure

**Building digital resilience:**
Tang promoted policies related to digital resilience, including cybersecurity, critical infrastructure protection, and digital sovereignty, ensuring Taiwan's security and autonomy in the digital age.

**Accelerating digital transformation:**
She drove society-wide digital transformation, helping businesses, civil organizations, and government agencies enhance their digital capabilities to meet the challenges of the digital era.

**Safeguarding digital human rights:**
Tang paid particular attention to digital human rights, promoting policies to protect citizens' rights in the digital world, including privacy protection and digital equity.

**International digital cooperation:**

### Outstanding Performance During the Pandemic

**Digital pandemic prevention innovations:**

## Philosophical Thought and Social Vision

### Conservative Anarchism as a Political Philosophy

**The unique stance of "conservative anarchism":**
Tang describes herself as a "conservative anarchist" -- a seemingly contradictory concept that reflects her distinctive political philosophy. She believes the best government is one that intervenes least, while conservatively preserving existing beneficial institutions.

**The ideal of minimal government:**
She maintains that the government's role should be to help citizens solve problems rather than making decisions for them. Technology should empower citizens, giving them greater autonomy and choice.

**Decentralized governance:**
Tang believes in decentralized governance, arguing that power should be distributed as widely as possible to enable more people to participate in decision-making.

**The importance of checks and balances:**
While advocating for minimal government, she also emphasizes the importance of checks and balances. Technology can help establish more transparent and accountable oversight mechanisms.

### The Vision of Digital Democracy

**The concept of "Code is Law":**
Tang has been deeply influenced by the idea that "code is law." She believes that in the digital age, code functions like legislation in governing behavior. Therefore, the process of writing code should, like the legislative process, be democratic and transparent.

**Commitment to technological neutrality:**
She insists that technology itself should remain neutral, free from particular value biases. Technology should serve everyone, not just specific interest groups.

**Participatory design in practice:**
Tang promotes the principle of participatory design, believing that any policy or system affecting citizens should involve them in the design process.

**The importance of digital literacy:**
She stresses the importance of digital literacy, believing that every citizen in the digital age should possess basic digital skills and a digital mindset.

### Perspectives on Education and Learning

**The value of self-directed learning:**
Drawing on her own experience, Tang deeply believes in the value of self-directed learning. She holds that the best learning is driven by intrinsic motivation rather than passive reception of knowledge.

**Recognition of multiple intelligences:**
She advocates that education should recognize multiple forms of intelligence and should not evaluate students by a single standard. Every individual has their own gifts and learning style.

**The necessity of lifelong learning:**
In the rapidly changing digital age, Tang emphasizes the necessity of lifelong learning. No one can stop learning, as knowledge and technology are constantly evolving.

**Open educational resources:**
She supports the concept of open educational resources, believing that knowledge should flow freely and should not be monopolized by commercial interests.

### Gender and Diverse Values

**Understanding the gender spectrum:**
Tang's experience with gender identity has given her a profound understanding of gender issues. She views gender not as binary but as a spectrum, and believes every individual should have the freedom to choose and express their gender identity.

**An inclusive society:**
She advocates building a diverse and inclusive society where people of different backgrounds and identities can live and work safely.

**A commitment to anti-discrimination:**
Through her own experience, Tang has proven that members of minority groups can play important roles in society. Her success provides a role model and source of hope for other LGBTQ+ individuals.

**Human rights as a universal value:**
She emphasizes that human rights are universal values -- regardless of gender, race, religion, or sexual orientation, every person should enjoy fundamental human rights protections.
