# Audrey Tang

Audrey Tang, a legendary figure who redefined political participation in the digital age. From a gifted teenager who started self-learning programming at 14 to becoming the world's first openly transgender cabinet minister, her life trajectory demonstrates Taiwan's diversity, inclusiveness, and innovative vitality. As a pioneer of the open government movement and co-founder of g0v (gov-zero), Audrey Tang not only reformed government operations with technical expertise but also practiced the "code is law" vision of digital democracy through her "conservative anarchist" philosophy. Her story is a modern legend about weaving a better society with code.

## Early Life and Unconventional Growth

### Genius Child's Struggles and Awakening

Born April 18, 1981, in Taiwan as Tang Tsung-han, Audrey demonstrated exceptional intelligence from an early age. Reportedly with an IQ exceeding 180, she was called "Taiwan's computer child." However, this gift didn't bring a smooth educational experience during her childhood.

**Difficult School Life:**
Being intellectually advanced beyond her peers, Audrey encountered serious difficulties interacting with classmates and teachers. She experienced bullying and couldn't adapt to the traditional education system. In just 9 years, she changed 3 kindergartens and 6 elementary schools, a painful experience that led to deep reflection on the education system.

**Mother's Progressive Education:**
Audrey's mother, Li Ya-ching, was an education reformer. Facing her daughter's adaptation difficulties, she decided to implement progressive home education. Li even took her two children to Germany to experience different educational environments, deeply studying alternative education methods, educational reform, and experimental education.

**Beginning of Self-Learning Path:**
After being qualified for direct admission to Jianguo High School, Audrey made a surprising decision: abandoning traditional school education to choose home learning. This decision drew considerable attention in Taiwan society at the time and opened her unique learning journey.

### Programming Enlightenment at 14

**First Encounter with Computers:**
At 14, Audrey was introduced to programming, beginning her deep connection with the digital world. She not only quickly mastered programming languages but also demonstrated profound understanding and innovative ability in computer science.

**Self-Taught Mastery Process:**
Without systematic teacher instruction, Audrey completely relied on self-learning to master programming essentials. She extensively read technical documentation, participated in online discussions, and sought learning resources in online communities. This autonomous learning experience laid the ideological foundation for her later promotion of open education and knowledge sharing.

**Early Technical Achievements:**
During her teenage years, Audrey was already participating in open-source software development. Her demonstrated technical talent and innovative thinking gradually made her stand out in the programming community.

### Gender Identity Exploration and Brave Coming Out

**Gender Identity Awakening:**
During her growth process, Audrey gradually realized her gender identity didn't match her assigned gender at birth. This recognition process wasn't easy, requiring courage to face social prejudice and potential misunderstanding from family and friends.

**Important Decision at 24:**
In 2005, at age 24, Audrey publicly declared her gender identity as "transgender woman" and underwent gender affirmation surgery. She changed her name from Tang Tsung-han to Tang Feng, choosing the English name Audrey. This decision required tremendous courage and determination.

**Significant Social Breakthrough:**
Audrey's public coming out had important demonstrative significance in Taiwan society at the time. Her actions proved that transgender people could also play important roles in society, making significant contributions to Taiwan's LGBTQ+ rights movement.

**Family Support:**
Fortunately, Audrey's family provided full understanding and support. Her mother Li Ya-ching's open-minded attitude provided important emotional support for Audrey's gender transition.

## Programming Career and Open Source Contributions

### Leadership in International Open Source Community

**Contributions to Perl Language:**
Audrey is a major contributor to the Perl programming language community. She participated in developing several important Perl projects, particularly making outstanding contributions to Perl 6 language design and implementation.

**Pugs Project Initiative:**
She initiated the Pugs project, an important implementation of the Perl 6 language. This project demonstrated her deep expertise in programming language design and established her position in the international programming community.

**CPAN and Package Management:**
Audrey contributed significantly to CPAN (Comprehensive Perl Archive Network) and various package management tools, improving the development experience for programmers worldwide.

### Entrepreneurship and Business Innovation

**Early Startup Experience:**
Before entering government service, Audrey founded several technology companies, including Socialtext, which focused on enterprise social networking and collaboration tools.

**International Consulting:**
She served as a consultant for various international technology companies, providing expertise in software architecture, open-source development, and digital transformation.

**Innovation in Business Models:**
Audrey pioneered new approaches to software development that emphasized transparency, collaboration, and community-driven innovation.

## g0v Movement and Civic Technology

### Co-founding g0v (gov-zero)

**Vision and Philosophy:**
In 2012, Audrey co-founded g0v (pronounced "gov-zero"), a civic technology movement aimed at promoting government transparency through technology and citizen participation. The movement's philosophy is "don't ask why nobody is doing this, you are the one to do it."

**Fork the Government:**
The g0v concept involves "forking" government websites and services to create improved, more transparent versions. This approach treats government as an open-source project that citizens can contribute to improving.

**Community Building:**
Under Audrey's leadership, g0v grew into a vibrant community of programmers, designers, journalists, and activists working together to solve social problems through technology.

### Key g0v Projects

**Budget Visualization:**
g0v created interactive budget visualization tools that made government spending more transparent and understandable to citizens.

**Legislative Transparency:**
Developed tools to track legislative proceedings, making parliamentary activities more accessible to the public.

**Disaster Response Systems:**
During natural disasters, g0v volunteers created crowdsourced information systems to help coordinate relief efforts.

## Digital Minister and Government Innovation

### Appointment as Digital Minister

**Historic Appointment:**
In 2016, at age 35, Audrey Tang was appointed as Taiwan's first Digital Minister (later Minister of Digital Affairs), becoming the world's first openly transgender cabinet minister.

**Unique Working Style:**
Audrey introduced radical transparency to government work, live-streaming meetings, publishing transcripts, and maintaining open office hours for citizen consultation.

**No Hierarchical Authority:**
She accepted the position with the condition of having no subordinates and no authority to give orders, preferring to work through collaboration and consensus-building.

### Major Government Initiatives

#### vTaiwan Platform

**Collaborative Governance:**
Developed vTaiwan, an online platform for collaborative policy-making that allows citizens to participate directly in government decision-making processes.

**Uber Regulation Case:**
Successfully used vTaiwan to resolve the contentious issue of Uber's legal status in Taiwan through stakeholder collaboration and consensus-building.

**Digital Economy Policies:**
Facilitated discussions on various digital economy regulations, from fintech to platform economy governance.

#### COVID-19 Digital Response

**Mask Availability System:**
During the COVID-19 pandemic, Audrey led the development of a real-time mask availability system that helped citizens locate pharmacies with masks in stock.

**Digital Fence Model:**
Implemented Taiwan's "digital fence" quarantine system using mobile phone location data instead of physical lockdowns.

**Rapid Response Innovation:**
Demonstrated how government could rapidly adapt and innovate during crisis situations using civic technology approaches.

#### Open Government Initiatives

**Radical Transparency:**
Promoted radical transparency in government operations, making meeting transcripts, decision-making processes, and policy deliberations publicly available.

**Citizen Participation Tools:**
Developed various digital tools for citizen participation in governance, including online petition platforms and policy co-creation mechanisms.

**International Collaboration:**
Shared Taiwan's digital governance innovations with international partners and participated in global digital democracy initiatives.

## Philosophy and Ideology

### Conservative Anarchism

**Political Philosophy:**
Audrey describes her political philosophy as "conservative anarchism" - using technology to preserve traditional values while reducing the need for hierarchical authority.

**Code as Law:**
Believes in the principle that "code is law," where well-designed systems and algorithms can create better governance outcomes than traditional regulatory approaches.

**Liquid Democracy:**
Advocates for liquid democracy models where citizens can delegate their decision-making power dynamically based on issue expertise.

### Technology for Social Good

**Human-Centered AI:**
Promotes development of AI systems that augment human capability rather than replace human judgment.

**Digital Rights:**
Champions digital rights and internet freedom as fundamental human rights in the digital age.

**Collective Intelligence:**
Believes in harnessing collective intelligence through technology to solve complex social problems.
