# Wu Ming-Yi

Wu Ming-Yi is contemporary Taiwan's most important nature writing author, a literary master who weaves ecological poetry through words. Born in 1971, he observes the intersection of nature and humanity through his unique compound eye perspective, creating unprecedented literary landscapes. Transforming from an entomologist to a literary creator, his works like "The Man with the Compound Eyes" and "The Stolen Bicycle" have not only caused sensation in the Chinese-speaking world but also made him the first Taiwanese author to be longlisted for the International Man Booker Prize. As a professor at National Dong Hwa University's Department of Chinese Literature, Wu Ming-Yi is not only a creator but also an important advocate for Taiwan's nature writing tradition, using literature to voice ecological conservation and showcase Taiwan literature's unique charm to the world.

## Early Life and Educational Background

### Growing Up in Taipei

Born June 20, 1971, in Taipei, Wu Ming-Yi spent his childhood and adolescence in the urban environment. However, unlike many children raised in concrete jungles, Wu from an early age harbored deep interest and keen observation of the natural world.

**Childhood Nature Enlightenment:**
Despite living in an urban environment, Wu displayed strong interest in insects and small animals from childhood. He frequently observed insects and plants near his home, and these early natural observation experiences planted important seeds for his later literary creation.

**Family Environment's Influence:**
Wu's family wasn't a literary household, but family members supported and tolerated his interests and hobbies. This free-growing environment allowed him to develop according to his own interests, unconstrained by traditional frameworks.

**Natural Experiences at Urban Edges:**
In Taipei's peripheral areas, Wu found many opportunities to contact nature. These natural spaces within the city later became important scenes and sources of inspiration in his literary works.

### Exploration During University Years

**Chinese Literature Studies:**
Wu chose Chinese Literature as his university major, which laid a solid foundation for his literary creation. During university, he not only studied traditional Chinese literature but also encountered modern literary theory and creative techniques.

**Literary Society Participation:**
During university, Wu actively participated in various literary society activities. He began attempting creative writing and published works in campus literary magazines. These early creative experiences significantly influenced the formation of his literary style.

**Interest in Natural Sciences:**
Besides literature, Wu maintained strong interest in natural sciences. He frequently read biology and ecology-related books, and this interdisciplinary knowledge structure provided scientific foundation for his later nature writing.

**Creative Philosophy's Germination:**
During university, Wu began contemplating how to combine his love for nature with literary creation. He realized that Taiwan literature lacked in-depth nature writing, which became an important direction for his future creative work.

### Graduate School Transformation

**Advanced Study in Chinese Literature:**
After university graduation, Wu entered graduate school to continue advanced study in Chinese literature. During graduate school, he studied literary theory more deeply, particularly modern literary criticism theory.

**Amateur Entomological Research:**
Besides formal literary research, Wu conducted amateur entomological research. He spent considerable time observing and recording various insects' ecological habits, and this scientific research experience provided unique perspectives for his literary creation.

**Combining Creation and Academia:**
During graduate school, Wu began contemplating how to combine academic research with literary creation. He realized that rigorous academic research methods could bring new possibilities to literary creation.

**Crucial Decision at 29:**
Wu once set himself a deadline: if he couldn't achieve results in writing by age 30, he would abandon creative work. Fortunately, at 29, "The Book of Lost Butterflies" (迷蝶誌) was published, and this book's success confirmed his creative path.

## "The Book of Lost Butterflies": Beginning of Nature Writing

### Creative Background and Motivation

**Fascination with Butterflies:**
The creation of "The Book of Lost Butterflies" originated from Wu's deep fascination with butterflies. He spent years studying Taiwan's butterfly ecology, accumulating vast observational data and profound insights.

**Fusion of Science and Literature:**
This book's greatest characteristic is its perfect fusion of scientific observation with literary writing. Wu used poetic language to describe butterfly ecological habits, creating entirely new writing modes.

**Mourning for Disappearing Nature:**
"The Book of Lost Butterflies" is not only praise for butterflies but also mourning for gradually disappearing natural environments. Wu recorded through words those beautiful lives that might soon vanish.

### Writing Style and Characteristics

**Poeticization of Prose:**
Wu demonstrated highly poeticized prose style in "The Book of Lost Butterflies." He used rich metaphors and symbolism, elevating butterfly observation to philosophical contemplation.

**Literaturization of Scientific Spirit:**
The book displayed scientific observation's rigor, but this scientific spirit was wrapped in literarized language, creating unique aesthetic effects.

**Integration of Interdisciplinary Knowledge:**
Wu integrated knowledge from biology, ecology, literature, philosophy, and other different fields, creating multi-layered textual meanings.

### Literary Response and Influence

**Milestone in Taiwan Nature Writing:**
"The Book of Lost Butterflies" is hailed as an important milestone in Taiwan nature writing. It initiated Taiwan literature's deep attention to natural environments and influenced many subsequent writers.

**Awards and Recognition:**
This book won multiple important literary awards and was selected as a book of the year. Its success helped Wu establish himself in literary circles.

**Beginning of International Attention:**
"The Book of Lost Butterflies" also began receiving attention from international literary circles. Some foreign nature literature researchers noticed this emerging writer from Taiwan.

**Establishment of Creative Model:**
This book established Wu's unique creative model: based on scientific observation, using literary techniques for in-depth writing. This model became the basic characteristic of all his subsequent works.

## Deepening and Expanding Nature Writing

### "The Butterfly Way": Exploring Ecological Philosophy

**From Observation to Speculation:**
"The Butterfly Way" (蝶道) continues and deepens "The Book of Lost Butterflies." If the latter emphasized observation and recording, the former emphasizes speculation and philosophical exploration.

**Constructing Ecological Ethics:**
In "The Butterfly Way," Wu began constructing his own ecological ethical views. He contemplated the relationship between humans and nature, exploring possibilities for sustainable development.

**Excavating Eastern Ecological Wisdom:**
Wu excavated much ecological wisdom from Eastern traditional culture in the book, providing ideological resources for modern environmental protection.

### "So Close to the Water": Attention to Urban Ecology

**Nature at Urban Edges:**
This book shifted attention from pure natural environments to natural spaces at urban peripheries. Wu discovered that even in highly urbanized environments, nature still possesses tenacious vitality.

**Possibilities for Human-Nature Coexistence:**
Wu explored possibilities for harmonious coexistence between humans and nature. He didn't simply criticize urbanization but contemplated how to leave space for nature within modern civilization.

**Relationship Between Memory and Landscape:**
The book deeply explored the relationship between memory and landscape. Wu believed that every place carries rich memories, and these memories constitute a place's soul.

### "Floating Light": Combining Photography and Literature

**Cross-Media Experimentation:**
"Floating Light" (浮光) represents Wu's important experiment in cross-media creation. He combined photography with literature, creating new art forms.

**Dialogue Between Visual and Text:**
In this book, photographic works and textual works form dialogical relationships. They aren't simple pairings but mutually complement and interpret each other.

**Contemplation of Moment and Eternity:**
Wu contemplated the relationship between moment and eternity through photography. Each photograph captures a moment, but text endows these moments with eternal meaning.

## Fiction Writing Breakthrough: From Prose to Fiction

### "Closed on Mondays": Initial Fiction Exploration

**Attempting Genre Transformation:**
"Closed on Mondays" (本日公休) represents Wu's important attempt at transforming from prose to fiction creation. This transformation wasn't easy, requiring him to master entirely new narrative techniques.

**Urban People's Living Conditions:**
In this short story collection, Wu focused on urban people's living conditions. He used delicate strokes to depict modern people's loneliness, anxiety, and desires.

**Combining Reality and Surreality:**
Wu began using surreal techniques in fiction. He skillfully combined reality with fantasy, rationality with sensibility, creating unique fictional styles.

### "The Magician on the Footbridge": Reconstructing Childhood Memories

**Memories of Zhonghua Shopping Center:**
"The Magician on the Footbridge" (天橋上的魔術師), set in the disappeared Zhonghua Shopping Center, reconstructed the author's childhood memories. This represents not only personal recollection but also collective memory reappearance.

**Use of Magical Realism:**
Wu employed magical realist techniques in this book. Reality and magic, history and imagination skillfully interweave in the story.

**Coming-of-Age Novel Qualities:**
This book possesses coming-of-age novel qualities, describing a child's growth experience in specific time and space. Through children's eyes, readers see a world full of miracles.

**Successful TV Adaptation:**
"The Magician on the Footbridge" was later adapted into a TV series, receiving widespread acclaim. This successful adaptation brought Wu's works broader attention.

### "Coordinates of Sleep": Excavating War Memories

**Reflection on Pacific War:**
"Coordinates of Sleep" (睡眠的航線), set against the Pacific War, explores war's devastation of humanity and environmental destruction. This marks Wu's first direct treatment of historical material.

**Multiple Narrative Techniques:**
The novel uses multiple narrative techniques, telling the same story from different perspectives. This narrative method increases story complexity and depth.

**Contrast Between Nature and War:**
Wu creates stark contrast between nature and war in the novel. War represents destruction and death, while nature represents life and hope.

**Assuming Historical Responsibility:**
Through this novel, Wu demonstrates his assumption of historical responsibility. He believes writers have the obligation to record and reflect on history, preventing tragedy's repetition.

## "The Man with the Compound Eyes": Classic Ecological Novel

### Creative Background and Conception

**Compound Eye Metaphor:**
The title "The Man with the Compound Eyes" (複眼人) comes from insect compound eye structure. Wu uses this metaphor to express the importance of observing the world from multiple perspectives.

**Concern for Environmental Crisis:**
The novel's creative background is deep concern for global environmental crisis. Wu wanted to warn humanity about possible serious consequences of environmental destruction through fictional stories.

**Combining Science Fiction and Reality:**
"The Man with the Compound Eyes" possesses science fiction qualities, but its foundation is profound understanding of real environmental problems. Wu uses imagination to amplify real environmental crises.

### Story Structure and Themes

**Weaving Multi-Line Narrative:**
The novel adopts multi-line narrative structure, skillfully weaving different storylines together. Each line represents different perspectives and positions.

**Integration of Indigenous Culture:**
Wu integrates Taiwanese indigenous cultural elements into the novel. He believes indigenous ecological wisdom holds important enlightening significance for modern society.

**Exploring Environmental Justice:**
The novel explores environmental justice issues. Wu points out that environmental destruction often causes greater harm to disadvantaged groups.

**Imagining Future Worlds:**
"The Man with the Compound Eyes" depicts a future world severely damaged by environmental destruction. This imagination carries strong warning significance.

### Literary Techniques and Innovation

**Application of Ecocritical Theory:**
Wu applies ecocritical theory in the novel. He focuses not only on environmental problems but also on reconstructing human-nature relationships.

**Poeticization of Language:**
Even in fiction, Wu maintains poeticized language style. His words are filled with beauty and imagination.

**Fusion of Cross-Cultural Elements:**
The novel fuses multiple cultural elements, including indigenous culture, modern urban culture, and international environmental concepts.

### International Influence and Evaluation

**Translation and Overseas Publication:**
"The Man with the Compound Eyes" has been translated into multiple languages and published in many countries. It introduced international readers to Taiwan's environmental problems and literary creation.

**Exemplar of Environmental Literature:**
This novel is hailed as an exemplar work of environmental literature. It proves that literature can play important roles in environmental protection.

**Object of Academic Research:**
"The Man with the Compound Eyes" has become the subject of numerous academic studies. Researchers analyze this work from different angles including ecocriticism, postcolonial theory, and science fiction literature.

**International Award Recognition:**
The novel won France's Prix du Livre Insulaire, representing important international literary recognition of Wu's creative achievements.

## "The Stolen Bicycle": Interweaving Personal and Historical

### International Man Booker Prize Breakthrough

**Historic Longlisting in 2018:**
On March 12, 2018, the International Man Booker Prize announced its longlist, with Wu Ming-Yi's "The Stolen Bicycle" (單車失竊記) listed among them, making him the first Taiwanese author longlisted for this prize. This news caused enormous sensation in Taiwan's literary circles.

**International Literary Prize Significance:**
The International Man Booker Prize is hailed as one of the most important literary awards in the English-speaking world. Wu's longlisting represents not only personal honor but also an important milestone for Taiwan literature's internationalization.

**Taiwan Literature's International Visibility:**
This longlisting greatly enhanced Taiwan literature's international visibility. Many foreign readers and publishers began paying attention to Taiwan's literary creation.

**Insistence on Sovereignty Disputes:**
During the longlisting process, the Man Booker Prize organizers had labeled Wu's nationality as "China, Taiwan." Wu sternly protested and insisted on correction to "Taiwan," demonstrating his insistence on Taiwan's sovereignty.

### Novel's Themes and Structure

**Personal Memory and Collective History:**
"The Stolen Bicycle" uses a stolen bicycle as a clue to explore relationships between personal memory and collective history. The novel skillfully connects personal experiences with Taiwan's historical changes.

**Intergenerational Transmission of War Trauma:**
The novel deeply explores how war trauma is transmitted between generations. Even though war has ended, its influence remains deeply imprinted in people's memories.

**Object Memory:**
Wu explores how objects carry memory through a bicycle. Every object has its own story and serves as a historical witness.

**Intersecting Multiple Time-Spaces:**
The novel uses intersecting multiple time-space narrative techniques. Past and present, reality and fiction skillfully interweave in the story.

### Writing Techniques and Style

**Investigative Report-Style Narration:**
The novel adopts investigative report-style narration. The protagonist, like a detective, gradually unveils secrets behind the bicycle theft.

**Use of Historical Materials:**
Wu extensively uses historical materials in the novel. These materials not only provide historical background but also increase story authenticity.

**Combination of Photos and Text:**
The novel intersperses many photos, creating dialogue between text and images. This cross-media expression increases work richness.

**Maintaining Poetic Language:**
Even when handling heavy historical material, Wu maintains poetic language style.

### International Response and Influence

**Translation Quality Importance:**
The English translation quality of "The Stolen Bicycle" is excellent, allowing foreign readers to fully experience the original work's charm. Excellent translation is important guarantee for work internationalization.

**Literary Criticism Recognition:**
Foreign literary critics gave this novel high praise. They appreciated Wu's unique narrative style and profound thematic content.

**Publishing Market Response:**
After overseas publication, the novel received good market response. Many bookstores listed it as recommended reading.

**Academic Attention:**
Foreign academic circles began paying attention to Wu's creative work. Many universities included his works as research subjects.
