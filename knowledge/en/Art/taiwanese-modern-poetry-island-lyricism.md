# Taiwanese Modern Poetry: Island Lyricism

The development of Taiwanese modern poetry is like a winding river, starting from its budding in the 1950s, passing through various periods of change and evolution, and finally converging into today's rich and diverse poetic landscape. This poetic path has not only witnessed the growth of Taiwanese literature but also reflected the spiritual face and cultural pursuits of an island amidst historical changes.

## Historical Origins of Modern Poetry

### Restarting in the Early Post-war Period

The real start of Taiwanese modern poetry can be traced back to the 1950s. In the early post-war period, as large numbers of literati and scholars moved to Taiwan, the tradition of Chinese modern poetry took root on the island once again. The poetic creation of this period primarily inherited the New Poetry tradition since China's May Fourth Movement but also began to integrate local Taiwanese life experiences.

**Ji Xian** is hailed as the "igniter of Taiwanese modern poetry." He founded the _Modern Poetry_ (現代詩) magazine in 1953, proposing the theory of "horizontal transplantation," advocating for learning the techniques and spirit of Western modernist poetry, and laying an important foundation for the development of Taiwanese modern poetry.

## The Golden Age of the Three Major Poetry Societies

From the mid-1950s to the 1970s, Taiwanese modern poetry entered a golden development period, marked most significantly by the establishment and development of the **Three Major Poetry Societies**: the **Modern Poetry Society**, the **Blue Star Poetry Society**, and the **Genesis Poetry Society**. These three societies each had their characteristics and jointly promoted the prosperity of Taiwanese modern poetry.

### Modern Poetry Society: Horizontal Transplantation and Modernism

The **Modern Poetry Society** (現代詩社) was established in 1956, led by Ji Xian. The society advocated for "horizontal transplantation," actively introducing Western modernist poetry and emphasizing the modernity and internationality of poetry.

**Representative Figures**:

- **Ji Xian** (1913-2013): The soul of the Modern Poetry Society, advocating the "Six Major Creeds."
- **Cheng Chou-yu** (1933-): Famous for works like _Mistake_ (錯誤), with an elegant and implicit poetic style.
- **Luo Xing**, **Yang Yun-da**, etc.

Cheng Chou-yu's _Mistake_ is considered a classic of Taiwanese modern poetry:

> I pass by Jiangnan / The face waiting in the seasons is like the blooming and falling of the lotus

This poem, with its beautiful imagery and rhythm, showcases the poetic aesthetics pursued by the Modern Poetry Society.

### Blue Star Poetry Society: Classical Sentiment and Modern Expression

The **Blue Star Poetry Society** (藍星詩社) was established in 1954, founded by Yu Kwang-chung, Qin Zi-hao, and others. Compared to the radical modernism of the Modern Poetry Society, the Blue Star Poetry Society focused more on the **integration of classical literary tradition with modern poetic expression**, pursuing "vertical inheritance."

**Representative Figures**:

- **Yu Kwang-chung** (1928-2017): A giant of Taiwanese modern poetry, author of _Homesickness_ (鄉愁).
- **Qin Zi-hao** (1912-1963): One of the founders of the Blue Star Poetry Society.
- **Zhong Ding-wen**, **Rong Zi**, **Luo Men**, etc.

Yu Kwang-chung's _Homesickness_ is one of the most widely circulated modern poems in the Chinese-speaking world:

> Homesickness is a shallow strait / I am on this end / The mainland is on that end

This poem not only showcases Yu Kwang-chung's profound literary skills but also reflects the national sentiment under a special historical background.

### Genesis Poetry Society: Surrealism and Experimental Spirit

The **Genesis Poetry Society** (創世紀詩社) was established in 1954, founded by Zhang Mo, Lo Fu, and Ya Hsien. The greatest feature of this society was its **strong experimental spirit**, actively exploring Western modernist techniques such as surrealism and symbolism.

**Representative Figures**:

- **Lo Fu** (1928-2018): The "Poetry Demon," who shocked the poetic world with _Death in a Stone Cell_ (石室之死亡).
- **Ya Hsien** (1932-): Author of _Like a Song-like Andante_ (如歌的行板).
- **Zhang Mo** (1931-2017): The organizer and promoter of the poetry society.

Lo Fu's _Gazing at My Home from the Border_ (邊界望鄉) showcases his profound literary achievement:

> Perhaps gold is buried there / Perhaps only / My mother is buried there

Ya Hsien's _Like a Song-like Andante_ is known for its musicality and rhythm:

> The necessity of tenderness / The necessity of affirmation / The necessity of a little wine and osmanthus flowers

## Local Turn: From Chinese Sentiment to Taiwanese Consciousness

Starting in the 1970s, Taiwanese modern poetry underwent an important turn. With the launch of the **Nativist Literature Debate**, poets began to focus more on local Taiwanese reality and culture, and poetic creation showed a trend toward **localization**.

### Wu Sheng: Nativist Poet of Soil Fragrance

**Wu Sheng** (1944-) is an important representative of Taiwanese nativist poetry. His poetry is deeply rooted in the soil and life of rural Taiwan, using plain language to express deep affection for his hometown.

Representative works include _Storefront_ (店仔頭) and _Speaking to Children_ (向孩子說). His verses often carry the fragrance of the soil:

> I am the son of a farmer / I deeply understand / The weight of every inch of land

Wu Sheng's poetry not only has a strong nativist color but also shows concern for environmental protection, earning him the title of "Taiwan's Poet of the Land."

### Xiang Yang: Pioneer of Taiwanese Language (Taigi) Poetry

**Xiang Yang** (1955-) is an important promoter of Taiwanese language modern poetry. He not only creates poetry in the Taiwanese language but also actively promotes Taiwanese literature. His work _Song of the Land_ (土地的歌) showcases the phonetic beauty and cultural connotation of Taiwanese language poetry.

Xiang Yang's contribution lies not only in creation but also in his construction and promotion of Taiwanese language poetic theory, opening a new path for the development of local Taiwanese poetry.

### Chen Li: Poetic Language Magician of Phonetic Experimentation

**Chen Li** (1954-) is one of the most innovative contemporary Taiwanese poets. His poetry has a strong experimental nature, good at using **sound**, **visuals**, and **word games** to create a unique poetic aesthetic.

His _Microcosmos_ (小宇宙) series and modern interpretations of classical poetry showcase his profound literary skills and innovative spirit. Chen Li is also an important translator, introducing a large amount of world poetry to Taiwan.

## The Rise of Female Poets

Female poets have also played an important role in the development of Taiwanese modern poetry, injecting different voices with their unique female perspectives.

### Xi Murong: Delicate Writing of Emotion

**Xi Murong** (1943-) is famous for her delicate writing of emotions. Her poetry often explores themes such as love, life, and nature. _A Blooming Tree_ (一棵開花的樹) is widely loved:

> How to make you meet me / At my most beautiful moment

### Yang Mu: Perfect Fusion of Classical and Modern

**Yang Mu** (1940-2020) is one of the masters of Taiwanese modern poetry. His poetic style is elegant, perfectly combining the deep heritage of classical literature with the expression techniques of modern poetry. Works like _Mountain Wind and Sea Rain_ (山風海雨) and _Time Propositions_ (時光命題) are classics of Taiwanese modern poetry.

## Diverse Development of Poetic Forms

### Rise of Prose Poetry

Starting in the 1980s, **prose poetry** gradually rose in the Taiwanese poetic world. This genre, somewhere between prose and poetry, provided poets with freer expressive space. Poets like **Luo Zhi-cheng** and **Chen Ke-hua** have made important contributions in this field.

### Concrete and Experimental Poetry

With the update of artistic concepts, new forms such as **concrete poetry** and **experimental poetry** appeared in Taiwan. These works broke the boundaries of traditional poetry, integrated elements of visual art, and showcased the multiple possibilities of poetry.

### Rise of Internet Poetry

At the beginning of the 21st century, the popularization of the Internet brought new changes to poetic creation and dissemination. New forms like **Internet poetry** and **blog poetry** emerged, providing young poets with new creative and publication platforms.

## Contemporary Voices

### Characteristics of the New Generation

Many excellent new-generation poets have emerged in contemporary Taiwan, showing the following characteristics:

1. **Bolder Language Experimentation**: New-generation poets are more daring in breaking the boundaries of traditional language.
2. **More Diverse Issues of Concern**: From environmental protection to gender, from globalization to local identity.
3. **More Innovative Use of Media**: Combining images, music, the Internet, and other multimedia elements.

**Representative Poets**:

- **Shao Xia**: Known for her unique language style.
- **Chen Yu-hong**: An important representative of female poets.
- **Ching Hsiang-hai**: A representative figure of the new-generation poets.

## Social Function and Cultural Significance

### Carrier of Cultural Inheritance

Taiwanese modern poetry has played an important role in cultural inheritance. From early Chinese cultural sentiment to later Taiwanese local consciousness, poetry has always been an important carrier of cultural identity.

### Pioneer of Language Experimentation

Poetry, as the highest form of language art, is often a pioneer of language experimentation. The development of Taiwanese modern poetry also reflects the evolution of the Chinese literary language.

### Care for Social Issues

Many poets care for social issues through their works, from early anti-communist literature to later nativist literature, from political criticism to environmental concern, poetry has always been closely related to the pulse of the times.

## International Exchange and Influence

### Translation and Promotion

The international promotion of Taiwanese modern poetry has also achieved certain results. Works by poets like Yu Kwang-chung, Yang Mu, and Chen Li have been translated into multiple languages, gaining attention in the international poetic world.

### Participation in International Poetry Festivals

Taiwanese poets actively participate in various international poetry festival activities, exchanging ideas with poets from all over the world, expanding the international influence of Taiwanese modern poetry.

## Challenges and Future Outlook

### Changes in the Reading Environment

With the diversification of entertainment, poetry faces the challenge of a decreasing readership. How to maintain the influence of poetry in the new media environment is a question that the contemporary poetic world must face.

### Balance Between Innovation and Tradition

How to find a balance between innovation and tradition, maintaining the modernity of poetry without losing its literary essence, is a question contemporary poets need to consider.

### Dual Demands of Localization and Internationalization

In the era of globalization, Taiwanese modern poetry must maintain its local characteristics while having an international vision. This dual demand forms the tension and challenge in creation.

## Conclusion: Sustainable Inheritance of Island Lyricism

From the foundation of the Three Major Poetry Societies to the deepening of the local turn, to contemporary diverse development, Taiwanese modern poetry showcases the rich changes of a literary genre in a specific time and space. Whether it's the homesickness of Yu Kwang-chung, the mistake of Cheng Chou-yu, the border of Lo Fu, or the soil of Wu Sheng, the Taiwanese language of Xiang Yang, the experiment of Chen Li, every voice has left a unique mark on the poetic map of this island.

These poems not only record personal emotions and thoughts but also witness the changes and development of Taiwanese society. They are like lighthouses on the island, emitting the light of humanity in the long river of history, guiding successors to continue forward.

The future of Taiwanese modern poetry will continue to develop in the dialogue between tradition and innovation, local and international. Just like the island itself, Taiwanese modern poetry will continue to showcase its unique charm and vitality in the fusion of diverse cultures. This island lyric tradition will surely bloom even more brilliant flowers in the new era.
